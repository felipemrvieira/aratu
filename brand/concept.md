# Aratu Brand Concept

## Name

**Aratu**

## Tagline

> Navigate complex data ecosystems.

## Concept

Aratu is a compact, capable navigator for environments defined by many connections. The name positions the product as attentive and adapted to complexity rather than dominant over it. In product terms, Aratu helps people move through schemas, relationships, records, and operational contexts while preserving awareness of risk.

## Mangrove Relationship

A mangrove is an estuary: roots, sediment, tides, organisms, and changing boundaries form one interdependent system. Databases behave similarly. Tables are not isolated surfaces; they participate in relationships, application workflows, permissions, environments, and downstream effects.

The aratu crab provides a useful behavioral metaphor: agile navigation through dense roots, sensitivity to local conditions, and a small footprint in a living ecosystem. Visual expression should remain abstract and professional rather than become a literal mascot.

## Brand Personality

- **Precise:** information hierarchy and language are unambiguous.
- **Calm:** risk is communicated clearly without alarm fatigue.
- **Observant:** context, relationships, and consequences remain visible.
- **Technical:** the product respects expert workflows and exposes generated SQL.
- **Responsible:** local-first behavior and review controls protect user trust.
- **Modern:** interaction and typography feel current without visual novelty obscuring data.

## Suggested Palette

The core palette combines dark tidal surfaces with mangrove green, estuary teal, reef cyan, and restrained sand-toned text. Coral and amber communicate destructive risk and caution. Exact tokens and usage are defined in `brand/colors.md`.

The Stitch source uses electric indigo, cyan, and emerald on deep neutral surfaces. Implementation should reconcile those references with the Aratu palette through semantic tokens: retain cyan/green state language and dark tonal depth, while using teal as the brand anchor. Indigo may remain a supporting focus/accent token until visual prototypes determine whether it is still necessary.

## Visual Use

- Prefer deep, layered surfaces and restrained borders for long technical sessions.
- Keep data, code, status, and diffs higher priority than decorative branding.
- Use generous workspace structure around dense tables rather than oversized cards.
- Use Geist for display/navigation, Inter for prose, and JetBrains Mono for SQL and identifiers, subject to license and bundling review.
- Use motion only to clarify state transitions, progress, or graph relationships; respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Logo Direction

The future logo should work as an app icon, small sidebar mark, monochrome glyph, and high-resolution brand lockup. Explore an abstract form combining mangrove roots, connected nodes, tidal channels, or the geometry of an aratu without using a detailed animal illustration.

Logo application rules:

- preserve clear space and legibility at 16-32 px;
- provide light, dark, monochrome, and platform-icon variants;
- do not use the logo as a database status indicator;
- avoid gradients or fine lines that disappear at application-icon sizes;
- validate trademark and cultural interpretation before final adoption.

No final logo asset has been selected in this phase.

## Relationship to Stitch

`docs/stitch/` is the primary UI reference for layout and interaction. It establishes the dark-first, premium technical character, sidebar workspace, tonal elevation, dense grids, and safety/review surfaces. The files use the former working name DataPilot and a preliminary palette. Aratu implementation should preserve the structural maturity while replacing naming and translating colors into the semantic system documented here.
